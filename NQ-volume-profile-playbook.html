NQ Scalping Playbook — 6 Setups

2 trend + 2 range from the CBOT floor tradition, plus 2 of my own setups. Every card: Context → Location → Entry → Confirmation → Stops & Targets.

1 point = $20
Tick = 0.25 ($5)
RTH = 9:30–16:00 ET
Best hours = 9:30–11:30 ET
Honest note: Win rates shown are commonly reported ranges from Market Profile literature and prop-desk practice — not guarantees. For my own two setups, the numbers are targets to verify. Log 100 tagged trades per setup; your journal overrides every number on this page.

STEP 1 Mark These Levels Before 9:30 ET

The complete map. All six strategies trade off these levels and nothing else.

Yesterday's RTH profile (9:30–16:00 ET): VAH, VAL, POC.Used by R1, R2, T1.
Overnight profile (18:00–9:30 ET): overnight high, low, POC.Breakout levels for T1.
Big composite profile — anchored at the first day of the current large multi-day range: composite VAH, VAL, POC + every HVN (fat zone) and LVN (thin gap).The map for T2 and MY-R.
Naked POCs from the last ~10 days within ~1% of price.Trend targets for T1, T2, MY-T.
Intraday: when price builds a fresh medium range (30–60 min), mark that range's developing VAH, VAL, POC on the session profile.The map for MY-T. After 10:30 also note the first-hour high/low for day type.
STEP 2 — Decide the day type before any trade:
RANGE day — overnight inside yesterday's range, value overlapping, narrow two-sided first hour → trade R1, R2, MY-R only.
TREND day — open drove away from value, first hour extended one way, one-sided delta → trade T1, T2, MY-T only.
Can't tell? No trades until it's obvious. Mixing range trades into a trend day is the account-killer.

RANGE Three Setups for Sideways Days

R1 fires multiple times per range day; R2 most weeks; MY-R whenever price reaches the extremes of a big balance.

R1 · RANGE2–4× PER RANGE DAY

Value Area Edge Fade — sell the top of value, buy the bottom

Win rate
~60–70%
Reward : Risk
1 : 1 – 1.5
Stop
10–15 pts
Targets
POC → far edge
Context
Range day only. Value overlapping yesterday's, overnight inside range, narrow two-sided first hour. Price rotates edge → middle → edge.
Location — profile to mark
Yesterday's VAH and VAL (entry zones) · yesterday's POC (target 1) · after 10:30 add today's developing VAH/VAL — where yesterday's edge and today's edge overlap is the strongest zone.
Entry
  1. Price pushes into VAL (long) or VAH (short); a small poke past the line is normal.
  2. Wait for the confirmation below — never enter on the touch alone.
  3. Enter at/just inside the edge on the delta flip.
Confirmation — order flow
Absorption + delta flip. At VAL: footprint shows heavy selling (clearly negative delta) but price stops going down — absorption. Next 1–2 bars flip to positive delta and lift off the low → enter. Mirror at VAH.
Kill switch: 15+ minutes of volume builds OUTSIDE the edge → range is breaking into trend. Stop fading, switch to T1.
R2 · RANGE2–3× PER WEEK

The 80% Rule — open outside value, accepted back in, ride across

Win rate (to POC)
~70–80%
Reward : Risk
1 : 1.5 – 3
Stop
10–15 pts
Targets
POC → far edge
Context
Open outside yesterday's value (NQ gaps often), then price pulls back inside and stays 15–20 min (acceptance). Once accepted, price usually crosses the whole value area.
Location — profile to mark
Yesterday's VAH, VAL, POC — nothing else. The edge price re-enters through = entry level · POC = target 1 · far edge = target 2.
Entry
  1. Open below VAL (long version; above VAH = mirror short).
  2. Price climbs inside value and holds 15–20 min — a quick in-and-out poke doesn't count.
  3. Price retests VAL from above; retest holds → long at VAL.
Confirmation — order flow
CVD divergence at the retest. Price makes an equal/slightly lower low at VAL but cumulative delta makes a higher low — sellers press and get nothing. Enter when bar delta turns positive off the level. Close back below VAL on negative delta = failed re-entry, no trade.
Kill switch: price drops back out of value and builds volume outside → the acceptance was fake, flat immediately.
MY-R · MY SETUPRANGE

Big Balance Extreme Fade — long the VAL / short the VAH of the BIG range

Win rate
Track your data
Reward : Risk
1 : 2 – 4
Stop
15–25 pts
Targets
Comp. POC → far edge
Context
Price is in balance inside a BIG multi-day range and has rotated all the way to one extreme of it — the composite VAL (for longs) or composite VAH (for shorts). The bigger the balance, the stronger the fade — these extremes are where the biggest players defend.
Location — profile to mark
Big composite profile (anchored at the first day of the large range): composite VAH (short zone) · composite VAL (long zone) · composite POC (target 1). Bonus: note the HVN nearest each extreme — price often stalls there on the way back.
Entry
  1. Confirm the big balance is intact (no acceptance outside it in recent days).
  2. Price reaches composite VAL (long) or composite VAH (short) — usually late in a rotation, often on a range day or a fading trend move.
  3. Wait for BOTH confirmations below — this is a big-level fade, so demand the most proof of any setup in the book.
  4. Enter at/just inside the extreme on the delta flip.
Confirmation — order flow (both required)
1 · Big-trade absorption: large prints hit the extreme aggressively (big sell market orders into VAL) but price stops moving — someone big is taking every contract.
2 · CVD divergence: price makes a new push low at VAL while cumulative delta makes a higher low (sellers exhausted). When both are on and bar delta flips positive → enter. One without the other = no trade.
Stops & targets
  • Stop: 15–25 pts beyond the absorption extreme (it must hold).
  • Target 1 (take most off): composite POC.
  • Runner: the opposite extreme of the big range.
Kill switch: price accepts beyond the big range edge (builds 15+ min of volume outside) → the entire balance is breaking. Exit instantly — that's the start of a real breakout, and the market becomes T1/T2 territory.

TREND Three Setups for One-Way Days

When NQ leaves value and drives — stop fading, start joining.

T1 · TREND1–2× PER TREND DAY

Break of Value + Retest — buy the first pullback to the broken level

Win rate
~50–60%
Reward : Risk
1 : 2 – 3
Stop
12–20 pts
Targets
NPOC → next HVN
Context
Trend day. Open drove away from value, one-sided delta since the open. Price breaks above value, returns once to the broken level, then continues. You buy the pullback — never the breakout itself.
Location — profile to mark
For longs: yesterday's VAH and the overnight high (whichever breaks = your level) · targets: nearest naked POC above + near edge of the next HVN on the composite. Mirror for shorts.
Entry
  1. Price breaks the level and HOLDS above — 10–15 min of volume builds above it.
  2. Wait for the first pullback down to the broken level.
  3. Pullback holds (confirmation below) → long at the level.
Confirmation — order flow
Weak pullback, strong return. Pullback arrives on shrinking volume and small delta (profit-taking, not real sellers). At the level, delta flips back positive — ideally with stacked buy imbalances as price turns. Heavy negative delta + rising volume into the level = real sellers, skip.
Kill switch: price closes back inside yesterday's value and stays → breakout failed, exit; the day may be turning into R1 conditions.
T2 · TREND1–3× PER TREND DAY

Thin Zone Break (LVN Air Pocket) — join the fast move through low volume

Win rate
~45–55%
Reward : Risk
1 : 2 – 4
Stop
12–20 pts
Targets
Next HVN edge → its POC
Context
Trend day only — never in midday chop. Price grinds at the edge of a fat zone (HVN) with a thin gap (LVN) directly beyond. Almost no business was done in the gap, so once it breaks, price shoots across to the next fat zone.
Location — profile to mark
On the composite profile: the HVN edge price is sitting at · the LVN just beyond it · the near edge + POC of the next HVN across the gap. Entry = start of the gap, target = far side.
Entry
  1. Stop order just past the LVN edge as the HVN gives way — or first tiny pullback after price enters the gap.
  2. Golden rule: enter at the START of the gap, never its middle.
Confirmation — order flow
Aggression through the edge. Delta strongly positive and GROWING, rising volume, stacked buy imbalances, no absorption capping it. Delta flat or volume dying on the poke = fake break, skip. Delta stalls mid-gap while in = take profit, exit.
Kill switch: price falls back inside the fat zone it broke from — out immediately, no hoping.
MY-T · MY SETUPTREND

Medium Range Acceptance Break — value accepted, then aggression at the top

Win rate
Track your data
Reward : Risk
1 : 2 – 3
Stop
12–18 pts
Targets
Measured move / NPOC
Context
Trend day, during a pause. Price builds a MEDIUM range — not a big multi-day balance, a 30–60 minute pause of roughly 20–40 NQ points — and value gets ACCEPTED inside it (a clear developing POC forms, price rotates around it). The trend is resting, not reversing. The break of this range with aggression is the next leg.
Location — profile to mark
The developing profile of the medium range itself: its VAH (the top — your trigger level for longs), its POC (must be clearly formed = acceptance; also your stop reference), its VAL. Targets: measured move (range height projected from the break) and the nearest naked POC / HVN edge beyond.
Entry
  1. Confirm the range is MEDIUM (20–40 pts, 30–60 min) — too small = noise, too big = it's a balance, use T2/MY-R logic instead.
  2. Confirm acceptance: a fat developing POC inside the range, price rotating around it. No POC = no acceptance = no trade.
  3. Watch the top of the range (VAH/range high) for the aggression signal below.
  4. Enter on the break of the range top with confirmation live — or on the first micro-pullback to the broken VAH.
Confirmation — order flow (both required)
1 · Big trades at the top: large aggressive prints lifting the offers at and through the range high — size stepping in, not small lots.
2 · Strong CVD: cumulative delta breaks to new highs WITH price (no divergence) and keeps climbing — the buying is real and persistent. Both on → enter. Big prints but flat CVD = one player, not a move — skip.
Stops & targets
  • Stop: 12–18 pts, below the range POC (back below the accepted value = the break failed).
  • Target 1: measured move — range height added to the breakout point.
  • Runner: nearest naked POC or next HVN edge above.
Kill switch: price breaks out, then closes back inside the medium range below its VAH — trapped breakout, exit immediately and stand down; the next signal is often the opposite-direction break.

STEP 4 Risk Rules

The setups don't work without this part.

Trade the good hours: 9:30–11:30 ET, optional 14:00–15:30. Skip lunch — levels stop working in thin volume.
Size from the stop: 15-pt NQ stop = $300/contract. Risk a fixed 0.25–0.5% per trade. Never pick contract size first.
Daily stop: 3 losers in a row OR −1.5R → done for the day, no exceptions.
Profile = location, order flow = trigger. The level says WHERE, the delta/absorption/CVD rule says WHEN. Take neither alone.
Journal every trade by tag (R1, R2, MY-R, T1, T2, MY-T). 100 tagged trades per setup = your real numbers.
