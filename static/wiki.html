Volume Profile & Order Flow Glossary

NQ Bias Engine Reference Guide

Point of Control (POC)

The Point of Control (POC) is the price level at which the greatest amount of volume was traded during the session. It represents the ultimate level of acceptance or "fair value" for the day. Traders use the POC as a powerful magnet; price frequently returns to this level during range-bound conditions.

Value Area High (VAH) & Value Area Low (VAL)

The Value Area defines the price range where 70% of the session's total volume was transacted. The upper boundary is the Value Area High (VAH), and the lower boundary is the Value Area Low (VAL). Prices inside the Value Area are considered fair, while prices outside are considered cheap (below VAL) or expensive (above VAH), triggering potential reversal or breakout trades.

Initial Balance (IB)

The Initial Balance (IB) is the price range established during the first 60 minutes of the regular trading session (typically 9:30 AM to 10:30 AM EST for US index futures). The high and low of this hour create structural boundaries. Breakthroughs of the IB boundaries often indicate the start of a strong trend day (T1 continuation setup).

Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) Divergence

Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) tracks the net difference between buying and selling pressure at the bid and ask. A **CVD Divergence** occurs when the price makes a new high/low, but the CVD fails to make a corresponding high/low. This indicates exhaustion or absorption by passive buyers/sellers, frequently signaling an imminent reversal (R1 reversal setup).

Single Prints & Excess

Single Prints are thin areas of volume on a profile chart where price moved rapidly, leaving behind low volume nodes. These act as support/resistance zones. **Excess** refers to rapid rejection at session highs or lows (buying/selling tails), confirming that institutional traders have rejected prices outside of value.
